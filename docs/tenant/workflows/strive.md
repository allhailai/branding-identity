# STRIVE (Promote) Workflow

**Transition to Self-Management**

## Overview

The **STRIVE workflow** (also referred to as the **Promote phase**) represents the successful transition of individuals from intensive care coordination to self-management with minimal support.

**ISJ Status:** `isj_promote`

**Primary owner:** STRIVE Specialists

**Goal:** Support individuals in maintaining stability independently while remaining available if they need to step back up to more intensive support

## Entry Criteria

**Individuals promoted to STRIVE when:**
- All criteria for promotion met (see [Stabilization workflow](./stabilization.md))
- Care team consensus that individual ready
- Individual expresses readiness and willingness

**Not everyone reaches STRIVE:** Some individuals may remain in Stabilize long-term due to ongoing high needs

## Transition Process

### Step 1: Warm Handoff to STRIVE

**From:** firsthand Guide and Health Guide
**To:** STRIVE Specialist

**Activities:**
1. Care team notifies individual of promotion (celebrate success!)
2. Introduce STRIVE Specialist
3. Explain what changes:
   - Less frequent check-ins (individual-driven vs team-driven)
   - STRIVE Specialist is main point of contact
   - HG still available for health visits as needed
   - Can step back up to more intensive support if needed
4. STRIVE Specialist meets individual (call or visit)
5. Transfer care plan and context

### Step 2: STRIVE Check-Ins

**Frequency:** Reduced from Stabilize
- Monthly or less frequent check-ins
- Individual-initiated contact encouraged

**Mode:** Primarily phone, occasional in-person if needed

**Activities:**
- "How are you doing?"
- Any new challenges or concerns?
- Celebrating continued progress
- Quick problem-solving if minor issues
- Escalate if more support needed

### Step 3: Self-Management Support

**Focus areas:**
- **Skills reinforcement:**
  - Scheduling own appointments
  - Refilling prescriptions
  - Navigating systems (benefits, healthcare, housing)
  - Problem-solving independently
- **Resource connection:**
  - Community resources for ongoing support
  - Peer support groups
  - Employment or education programs
  - Social activities and connections
- **Confidence building:**
  - Acknowledge growth and progress
  - Encourage taking on new challenges
  - Normalize setbacks as part of journey

### Step 4: Monitoring from Distance

**STRIVE Specialist monitors:**
- Claims data for hospitalizations or ER visits
- Appointment attendance (if visibility into scheduling)
- Individual's self-reported status

**No news is good news:** If individual not reaching out and no alerts, assume doing well

## Stepping Back to Stabilize

**Triggers for return to Stabilize:**
- **Hospitalization or multiple ER visits**
- **Housing loss or major life stressor**
- **Behavioral health crisis**
- **Individual requests more support**
- **Medication non-adherence leading to decompensation**

**Process:**
1. STRIVE Specialist identifies need
2. Discusses with individual
3. Reassigns to fG and HG
4. ISJ status: `isj_promote` → `isj_stabilize`
5. Service level set (likely high risk initially)
6. Intensive support resumed

**Not a failure:** Cycling back is expected and part of the model. Chronic conditions and social challenges are not linear.

## Health Guide Visits

**Individuals in STRIVE still need clinical care:**
- HG visits as needed for health management
- Frequency determined by medical needs, not care coordination intensity
- Individual schedules own appointments (with support if needed)
- HG continues HCC coding and HEDIS gap closure during visits

## Sustained Promotion

**Success:** Individual maintains stability in STRIVE for 6-12+ months

**Indicators:**
- No hospitalizations or ER visits
- Stable housing, benefits, health
- Independent management of health and social needs
- Engaged with community resources
- Positive quality of life

**Long-term:** Some individuals may remain in STRIVE indefinitely with minimal touch points

**Discharge:** In some models, individuals may "graduate" from program entirely if health plan allows

## Key Metrics

- **Promotion rate:** % of panel promoted annually
- **Sustained promotion rate:** % still in STRIVE after 6, 12 months (not cycled back)
- **Utilization in STRIVE:** Hospitalizations, ER visits vs Stabilize phase
- **Time to return:** For those who cycle back, how long were they promoted?
- **Quality of life:** Self-reported wellbeing in STRIVE

## Platform Implementation

**ISJ workflow status:** `isj_promote`

**STRIVE panel:** STRIVE Specialists have panel of promoted individuals

**Monitoring:** Automated alerts for hospitalizations or ER visits

**Tasks:** Light-touch check-in tasks, lower frequency

---

**Key Takeaway**: STRIVE represents the aspirational outcome of Firsthand's model - individuals empowered to manage their health and life independently, with a safety net available if they need more support. Not everyone reaches STRIVE, and that's okay; the model flexes to meet individuals where they are.
