# Firsthand Organizational Structure

**Teams, Roles, and Reporting Lines**

## Overview

Firsthand operates with **clinical and operational dyads** across the organization, pairing clinical expertise with operational leadership at every level to support integrated care delivery.

## Leadership

### Executive Leadership
- **Operations Officer**: Overall operational leadership
- **Clinical Officer**: Overall clinical strategy and quality
- **Medical Officer / PC President**: Physician leadership, clinical protocols

### Care Model Council
Cross-functional leadership team setting care model standards and clinical policies.

## Care Teams

### Community Care Teams

**Geographic organization:** Teams organized by state/region and local offices ("pods").

**Regions (example):**
- Nashville
- Knoxville
- Other states (WA, MI, OH expanding)

**Roles:**

#### Executive Director (ED)
- **Formerly:** SDCO (State Director of Clinical Operations)
- **Scope:** Owns P&L, clinical operations, and client success for their state
- **Responsibilities:**
  - State-level performance and outcomes
  - Team hiring and development
  - Health plan relationship management
  - Budget ownership

#### Manager of Community Operations (MCO)
- **Reports to:** Executive Director
- **Scope:** One or more local offices/pods
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Team lead and partnership builder
  - Assigns individuals to firsthand Guides (capacity management)
  - Ensures high standard of care delivery
  - Operational problem-solving
  - Performance coaching

#### firsthand Guide (fG)
- **Role type:** Peer support specialist (person with lived experience)
- **Reports to:** MCO
- **Responsibilities:**
  - **First contact** with individuals
  - Build trust and rapport
  - Conduct engagement visits (home/community)
  - Obtain consents
  - Conduct welcome sessions and ongoing outreach
  - Schedule and coordinate Health Guide visits
  - Navigate social determinants of health (housing, food, transportation)
  - Advocate for individual needs
- **Caseload:** Varies by individual complexity and risk level

#### Health Guide (HG) - Community
- **Role type:** Nurse Practitioner
- **Reports to:** Clinical Manager
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Conduct Whole Person Assessments (WPA)
  - Provide primary and behavioral health care
  - Prescribe medications
  - Create and monitor care plans
  - Conduct Individual Progress Reviews (IPR)
  - Determine risk stratification and service level
  - Support fG with clinical guidance
- **Panel size:** Varies by visit frequency needs

#### Clinical Manager (CM)
- **Reports to:** VP, Care Model Performance
- **Scope:** One or more states/regions
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Clinical supervision of Health Guides
  - Quality assurance
  - Clinical training and development
  - Care model adherence
  - Outcomes monitoring

#### Health Guide Assistant (HGA)
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Scheduling support for Health Guides
  - Answer triage line (initial assessment)
  - Route escalations to appropriate clinician
  - Administrative support for care team

### Central Care Teams

**Centrally located teams supporting all community pods.**

#### Psychiatric Nurse Practitioners (Psych NP)
- **Reports to:** VP, Central Care Ops Teams
- **Responsibilities:**
  - SOAR visits (psychiatric evaluation for benefits eligibility)
  - Behavioral health visits for high-risk individuals
  - Medication management for complex psych conditions
  - Support Health Guides with behavioral health consultation
- **Assignment:** May be assigned as "preferred Psych NP" after SOAR visit

#### Triage Registered Nurses (Triage RN)
- **Reports to:** VP, Central Care Ops Teams
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Answer triage line escalations
  - Assess individual needs remotely
  - Provide immediate support if able
  - Schedule with Health Guide if escalation needed
  - Conduct Transitions of Care (TOC) visits (post-hospitalization)
  - Close HEDIS gaps during TOC visits if dual-certified

#### SOAR Specialists
- **Reports to:** VP, Central Care Ops Teams
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Expert in SSI/SSDI application process
  - Work with individuals qualified for SOAR
  - Conduct medical eligibility determination
  - Complete SSA application drafts
  - Submit applications to local SSA offices
  - Track application status and appeals

#### STRIVE Specialists
- **Reports to:** VP, Central Care Ops Teams (or Operations)
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Support individuals ready for self-management
  - Less intensive touch points
  - Help with initial outreach for stable members
  - Transition planning

### Support Operations Teams

**Teams working behind the scenes to enable care delivery.**

#### Medical Records Specialists
- **Reports to:** VP, Central Care Ops Teams
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Request medical records from facilities (hospitals, PCPs, specialists)
  - Track request status and follow-up attempts (fax, phone)
  - Upload received records to EMR (Elation)
  - Close out requests after 30 days or when received

#### CDI Specialists (Clinical Documentation Improvement)
- **Reports to:** Coding/Billing leadership (may be under Tara)
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Prospective review of ICD-10 codes before visits
  - Publish review to clinicians in "Sidecar"
  - Retrospective review of visit documentation
  - Assign ICD-10 codes based on clinical documentation
  - Track diagnosis gap closure
  - Train clinicians on documentation best practices

#### Coding / Billing Team
- **Leadership:** Kandace Dalton (mentioned in org chart)
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Submit claims to health plans
  - HCC coding for risk adjustment
  - Revenue cycle management
  - Audit preparation and response

#### BenSupp (Benefits Support) - If separate from SOAR
- Assist with benefit enrollment and navigation beyond SSI/SSDI

## Office / Pod Structure

**Pod:** Local care team unit, typically consisting of:
- 1 MCO (manager)
- 3-7 firsthand Guides
- 1-2 Health Guides (may be shared across pods or virtual)
- Shared access to central teams (Psych NP, Triage RN, SOAR, Medical Records, CDI)

**Capacity:** Each pod serves ~150-300 individuals depending on risk mix.

## Reporting Lines Summary

```
Executive Leadership
├── Operations Officer
│   └── VP, Care Model Performance (Community)
│       ├── Clinical Managers
│       │   └── Health Guides (Community)
│       └── Executive Directors
│           └── MCOs
│               ├── firsthand Guides
│               └── HGAs
│
├── Clinical Officer
│   └── VP, Central Care Ops Teams
│       ├── Psych NPs
│       ├── Triage RNs
│       ├── SOAR Specialists
│       ├── Medical Records Specialists
│       └── [STRIVE if under central clinical]
│
└── [Other VPs: Finance, People, IT, Business Ops]
```

## Key Dyad Partnerships

**ED ↔ Clinical Manager:** State-level operations + clinical quality
**MCO ↔ HG:** Pod-level care coordination + clinical care
**fG ↔ HG:** Individual-level advocacy + medical care

---

**Key Takeaway**: Firsthand's structure emphasizes **clinical-operational partnerships** at every level, with community teams delivering care locally and central teams providing specialized support. The platform must support this distributed, role-based care model.
